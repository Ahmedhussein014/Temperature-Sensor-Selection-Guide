RTD Advantages & Limitations

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Excellent accuracy (¬±0.1¬∞C possible)
  • Superior long-term stability
  • Good linearity
  • Interchangeable without calibration
  • Wide temperature range (-200 to 850¬∞C)
  • Low drift over time

‚ùå Limitations

  • Self-heating effects
  • Requires excitation current
  • Slower response than thermocouples
  • Lead resistance compensation needed
  • More expensive than thermocouples
  • Fragile at high temperatures

Connection Configurations

2-Wire Configuration

Simple but includes lead resistance errors. Suitable for short distances and less accurate applications.

3-Wire Configuration

Most common in industry. Compensates for lead resistance. Recommended for most applications.

4-Wire Configuration

Most accurate. Eliminates lead resistance errors completely. Recommended for Class AA and A sensors.
